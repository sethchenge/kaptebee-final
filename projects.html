Project Details
Solar Powered Water Purification System

Project Overview

This innovative project addresses the critical issue of clean water access in rural communities. The team designed a sustainable solar-powered system that can purify up to 20 liters of water per day using locally available materials and renewable energy.

Problem Statement

Access to clean drinking water remains a significant challenge for many rural communities in our region. Traditional water purification methods are often expensive, require electricity, or utilize chemicals that may not be readily available.

Solution

Our system uses a combination of solar distillation and filtration technologies to purify water from local sources. The process involves:

  1. Initial filtration through locally sourced sand and charcoal
  2. Solar distillation chamber that evaporates and condenses water
  3. UV light exposure powered by small solar panels to kill remaining pathogens
  4. Storage in a clean container with controlled dispensing mechanism

Impact

The project has been successfully implemented in three neighboring communities, providing clean water to approximately 150 people daily. Local NGOs have provided funding to scale the project to additional communities.
